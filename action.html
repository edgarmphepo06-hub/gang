About NADA

Next Level Advanced Digital Administration (NADA) is a Malawian digital initiative established to promote innovation, structured administration, and technological advancement. It originated at William Murray Secondary School, an institution under the Nkhoma Synod.

Founding History & Early Establishment

NADA was conceptually proposed by Edgar Mphepo during Form 3 on 06 June 2024 at 19:48:36 hrs. It began as a student-driven initiative focused on digital innovation and structured administrative systems.

Eugene Banda served as the first recorded witness during the early conceptual stage and contributed to foundational discussions during the formation phase.

The initiative was officially announced on 18 June 2024 at 18:30 hrs, marking its formal establishment within the academic environment.

Recruitment was conducted through a structured interview process where applicants were assessed based on defined criteria. Those who did not meet requirements were formally informed, ensuring transparency and discipline in selection.

During its early phase, NADA operated under a controlled and discreet framework to maintain focus, discipline, and structured growth. This allowed selective integration of committed individuals while encouraging external observation and contribution of ideas.

Today, NADA continues to grow as a structured digital organization focused on delivering innovative solutions in administration, networking, and digital systems development.
